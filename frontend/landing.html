How This Estimate Works

What is this estimate?

This tool analyzes your Terraform configuration and provides a monthly cost estimate based on publicly available cloud provider pricing. It's designed to help you understand costs before deployment, when you can still make changes to your infrastructure design.

How are costs calculated?

Costs are calculated using: Cloud provider pricing APIs for current rates, Resource configurations from your Terraform code (instance types, storage sizes, etc.), and Reasonable assumptions for usage patterns (like 730 hours/month for compute instances). All assumptions are clearly shown in the estimate.

What assumptions are involved?

Common assumptions include: compute instances running 24/7 (730 hours/month), default data transfer patterns, standard storage configurations, and provider-default regions when not specified. These assumptions are explicitly listed for each resource, so you can see what's included in the estimate.

What happens when I change a scenario?

Scenario comparisons let you explore "what if" questions: different regions, autoscaling patterns, or traffic assumptions. The base estimate never changes‚Äîscenarios create a new estimate alongside it, showing the cost difference (delta). This helps you understand how different choices affect costs without losing your original estimate.

What this is not

This is not an official cloud provider bill, a cost optimization tool, or a billing system. It's an estimation tool for planning and team conversations. Actual costs will vary based on real usage, reserved instances, discounts, and other factors not captured here.

How should I use this?

Use this during infrastructure design and planning to: understand cost implications before deployment, have informed conversations with your team about trade-offs, explore different scenarios safely, and share estimates with stakeholders who need cost visibility. Think of it as a conversation starter, not a final answer.
